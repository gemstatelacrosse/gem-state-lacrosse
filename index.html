New to Lacrosse

Lacrosse is one of the fastest-growing sports in the Treasure Valley, and kids can start as early as kindergarten. Here is what families need to know to get started.

Pathway

Where does my kid fit?

Most Treasure Valley players start in TVYLL (Treasure Valley Youth Lacrosse League) and then move into IMSLA and high school leagues.

Level Grades Notes
TVYLL K–6 (boys), K–8 (girls) Recreational, learn fundamentals, local teams
IMSLA 6–8 (boys) School-based middle school league for boys
High school 9–12 SWILA (boys) and TVWLL (girls) high school leagues

*Some IMSLA programs allow 5th graders; contact your local program for details.

Overview

What is lacrosse?

Lacrosse is a fast-paced field sport that blends elements of hockey, soccer, and basketball using a stick with a mesh pocket to carry and pass a small rubber ball. [file:1]

  • Boys lacrosse is a contact sport with full protective gear. [file:1]
  • Girls lacrosse has limited contact and lighter required equipment. [file:1]
  • Games emphasize running, teamwork, and decision-making. [file:1]

What does it cost?

Costs vary by age, division, and program, but most families will see similar categories of expenses.

  • TVYLL registration typically ranges from about 150–300 dollars depending on age and division.
  • Additional costs can include jerseys for new players, refundable volunteer fees (up to 50 dollars), and a small processing fee.
  • Gear can often be reused for several seasons and passed down between siblings. [file:1]

Each program sets its own pricing, so always check your local program’s website or registration page for exact fees.

What gear do they need?

Boys gear checklist

  • Helmet (NOCSAE certified)
  • Shoulder pads (ND200 standard)
  • Arm pads and gloves
  • Stick
  • Cup and mouthguard
  • Cleats (recommended)

Girls gear checklist

  • Goggles (ASTM certified)
  • Women’s stick
  • Colored mouthguard (not clear)
  • Cleats (recommended)

Girls lacrosse usually requires less gear and is more affordable to start. [file:1]

Where to buy (or rent at LaxID)

  • Local: LaxID (purchase or rental), Dick’s Sporting Goods, Scheels
  • Online: Lacrosse Monkey, Amazon
  • Used: SidelineSwap, Facebook Marketplace, TVYLL gear swaps
Tip: Ask your program about loaner equipment, gear swaps, and sibling discounts before you buy everything new.

Frequently asked questions

What age can kids start lacrosse in the Treasure Valley?

Kids can start as young as kindergarten (age 5) in TVYLL’s 8U division, where the emphasis is on fun, fundamentals, and learning to love the game. [file:1]

My kid’s school doesn’t have a lacrosse team. Can they still play?

Yes! TVYLL teams are organized by geography (zip code) and also by nearest public high school depending on numbers. Your child will be placed on a team with other kids from your area. For middle school (IMSLA), you can petition to play for a nearby school’s team if yours doesn’t have one.

We homeschool or go to private school. Can we play?

Absolutely. TVYLL accepts players from all school types and places them on teams based on where they live, just like everyone else. [file:1]

Are scholarships available?

TVYLL has scholarships available for families who need financial assistance. For other teams and programs, you should contact the organization directly to ask about their scholarship or fee‑reduction options.

Programs

These are the main leagues and school-based programs serving the Treasure Valley. Always check each organization’s website for the most current information.

Youth Rec

Treasure Valley Youth Lacrosse League (TVYLL)

Co-ed youth league for boys (K–6) and girls (K–8) with local teams across Boise, Meridian, Eagle, Star, Kuna, Nampa, and surrounding communities. [file:1][web:4]

  • Divisions: 8U, 10U, 12U, 14U (girls)
  • Spring is the primary season, with some fall opportunities

Visit TVYLL website

Middle School

Idaho Middle School Lacrosse (IMSLA)

School-based league for boys in grades 6–8, preparing players for high school lacrosse. Teams are typically associated with local middle schools or feeder patterns. [file:1]

*Some programs allow 5th graders; contact your local program for details. IMSLA does not currently have a standalone website.

High School – Boys

Southwest Idaho Lacrosse Association (SWILA)

SWILA is the governing league for boys high school lacrosse in the Treasure Valley. Teams include Bishop Kelly, Boise, Rocky Mountain, Eagle, Mountain View, Timberline, Centennial, and others. [file:1][web:9]

Visit SWILA website

High School – Girls

Treasure Valley Women’s Lacrosse League (TVWLL)

TVWLL organizes girls high school lacrosse programs in the Treasure Valley, with teams aligned to local high schools. [web:5][web:11]

Parents

Whether you are new to the sport or transitioning from another activity, this section is designed to help parents understand expectations and resources.

What to expect

  • Most youth teams practice 2–3 times per week during the spring season.
  • Games are typically on Saturdays, with some weeknight games for older divisions.
  • Volunteer coaches and team parents are a huge part of making lacrosse possible.

Helpful resources

  • USA Lacrosse parents resources and rules overview: usalacrosse.com
  • Mountain Lacrosse Network (MLN Idaho) – local game streams and highlights: YouTube @MLNIdaho

Community & Officiating

Lacrosse in Idaho is driven by volunteers, officials, and community partners working together to grow the sport.

Idaho Lacrosse Officials Association (ILOA)

ILOA trains and assigns officials for youth and high school games across Idaho, helping ensure safe, consistent play and development opportunities for new referees. [web:18]

Visit idlaxref.org

Volunteer opportunities

TVYLL, IMSLA, and high school programs are all heavily volunteer‑dependent. Teams rely on coaches, team managers, clock and table staff, and board‑level leadership.

To get involved, contact your specific program or email gemstatelacrosse@gmail.com and we can help connect you.
